Child Protection & Safeguarding Policy

Every child at Ambassadors has the right to be safe, respected and heard.

Last updated: 1 May 2026

🛡️ Our commitment

Ambassadors Educational Complex is committed to creating a safe, caring and disciplined environment in which every child can learn, play and grow without fear. We adopt a zero-tolerance approach to any form of abuse, bullying or neglect.

1. Scope

This policy applies to all learners, teaching and non-teaching staff, volunteers, contractors, parents and visitors. It complements national legislation, including Cameroon's laws on the rights and protection of the child.

2. Principles

  • The welfare of the child is paramount.
  • Every child is treated with dignity, regardless of background, gender, religion or ability.
  • All staff are responsible for safeguarding.
  • Concerns are taken seriously and acted on promptly.

3. Recruitment and training

  • All staff undergo background checks before employment.
  • Annual safeguarding training is mandatory.
  • A Designated Safeguarding Lead (DSL) coordinates all child protection matters.

4. Code of conduct

  • Staff–learner communication outside school must use official channels only.
  • Physical punishment is strictly forbidden.
  • One-to-one meetings happen in visible spaces.
  • Staff dress and language remain professional at all times.

5. Reporting concerns

Any child, parent or staff member who is worried about a learner's safety can:

  • Speak to any teacher or directly to the Designated Safeguarding Lead.
  • Send a confidential email to safeguarding@ambassadors-edu.cm.
  • Use the suggestion box at the school reception.
  • Call +237 677 637 429.

All reports are handled with confidentiality, taken seriously and investigated promptly.

6. Online safety

Our digital tools are filtered and monitored. We educate students on responsible use of social media, cyberbullying prevention and password security.

7. Anti-bullying

Bullying — verbal, physical or online — is unacceptable. Any incident triggers an investigation, support for the affected learner and restorative action.

8. Health, hygiene and emergencies

The school clinic is staffed by a qualified nurse during school hours. Emergency contacts are kept up to date for every learner. Fire drills are conducted termly.

9. Review

This policy is reviewed annually by the school's leadership team and the Parent–Teacher Association.

10. Contact our Designated Safeguarding Lead
